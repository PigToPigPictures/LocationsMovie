An American man, Arnold, follows his pregnant girlfriend, Camille, to the North of France to meet his new extended family. As he struggles to assimilate into his new life, his own paranoia is exacerbated by local folklore— suggesting that any soul unable to adapt to local customs is doomed to be entrapped in the stone by the sea.

Arnold’s violent past will come creeping to the surface as his fear of fatherhood will guide his every misstep. The family will be forced to reconcile, what should be done with this outsider’s negative energy?

The overall arc of the Locations narrative will follow one man's fall from grace with the entire village, who sought to recognize his toxicity and expel it from their lives, occupying the spaces between.

Can Arnold get out of his own way in time to see the birth of his child, or will the entire world come crashing down with him?

Blending seaside horror with raw emotional depth, Locations examines toxic masculinity, the weight of responsibility, and the ghosts we carry within.

Through its haunting atmosphere and profound storytelling, the film takes viewers on an unforgettable journey of self-discovery.
